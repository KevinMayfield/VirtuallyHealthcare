Virtually Healthcare Health Information Exchange (HIE)

Is a series of adaptors to other systems such as GP Systems and NHS England Services. The adaptors provide a layer of consistency and simplification. This means an application developers doesn’t have to deal with health and technical complexities.

Use Case Focused Solutions

We aim to support the following use cases:

  • Find Patient Find a patient record on a connected system.
  • Update Patient Demographics Update patient demographic records on a connected system.
  • Find Patient clinical notes and documents on connected systems and view them.
    • Clinical Notes and Compositions Handling of health specific notes/documents formats.
  • Query (structured) clinical record from connected systems. This is our preferred option for handling clinical records (not clinical notes or composition formats).

To do this we use a mix of Enterprise, Technical and Data Standards.

Enterprise/Business Standards

The following standards describe how HL7 FHIR is used for common use cases.

  • IHE Query for Existing Data mobile (QEDm)
    • or International Patient Access
  • IHE Mobile access to Health Documents (MHD)
    • This is the replacement of legacy XDS XML+SOAP API’s on IHE XDS systems
  • IHE Mobile Care Services Discovery (mCSD)
  • IHE Patient Demographics Query for mobile (PDQm)
  • FHIR Structured Data Capture (SDC)
    • A modern version of IHE Mobile Retrieve Form for Data Capture

Technincal Standards

All the Enterprise/Business standards use the same HL7 FHIR schema and are built on FHIR RESTful API’s (FHIR version is R4).

Most of our adaptors interface with other standards. Other standards we support (or can support) include:

  • GP Systems IM1
  • GP Systems PFS
  • IHE XDS
  • HL7 v2 ADT and ORU
  • openEHR
  • NHS England (PDS, MESH and NRLF)

Data Standards

Our HIE and CDR is compliant with NHS Data Dictionary and Model and UK SNOMED CT requirements. Our data is also HL7 UK Core FHIR conformant. At present most of our adaptors only perform minimal code conversions to provide a pragmatic level of commonality between the different systems.
